## Critique

**Overall:** Well-organized and readable, but reads more like a wellness brochure than a rigorous summary. The claims range from well-supported to overstated.

---

**Strengths**
- Clear categorical structure makes it easy to scan
- Closing sentence about short sessions is practically useful and grounded
- Covers a reasonable breadth of domains

---

**Weaknesses**

**Overclaiming without qualification**
- "Strengthened immune function" — evidence is weak and inconsistent; this shouldn't sit alongside better-supported claims without a caveat
- "Structural brain changes (increased gray matter)" — often cited, but many source studies have small samples, no active control groups, and replication is mixed
- "Slowed age-related cognitive decline" — emerging area with promising but preliminary evidence; presenting it as settled is misleading

**Vagueness that obscures meaning**
- "Lower cortisol levels" — cortisol varies throughout the day and context; in what conditions, measured how?
- "Improved relationships through better empathy and patience" — this is inferential, not directly measured
- "Greater life satisfaction and sense of purpose" — self-reported outcomes are meaningful, but the phrasing implies objective measurement

**Missing nuance**
- No mention that effect sizes vary significantly by meditation type (mindfulness vs. transcendental vs. loving-kindness, etc.)
- No acknowledgment that meditation can have adverse effects for some people (anxiety, depersonalization, surfacing trauma)
- "Even short sessions... within a few weeks" — true for some outcomes (mood, stress), not for structural brain changes

**Structural issue**
- "Long-term" as a category mixes timescales arbitrarily — brain changes require years of practice, while cognitive decline is a lifetime metric. Grouping them implies they're on the same timeline.

---

**Recommendation**

Add qualifiers like *"some studies suggest"* or *"in regular, long-term practitioners"* to the weaker claims. Separate well-replicated findings from emerging or preliminary ones. A single sentence acknowledging heterogeneity of evidence would significantly improve credibility without undermining the piece's usefulness.
