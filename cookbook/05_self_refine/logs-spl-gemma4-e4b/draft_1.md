## 🛠️ Optimization Pillars (Minor Edits for Polishing)

These suggestions are not fixes, but advanced techniques to elevate the polish and preempt reader friction.

### 1. The Hook & Narrative Arc (Rhetoric)
*   **Objective:** Intensify the emotional urgency at the outset.
*   **Tweak:** In the Blog Post introduction, move beyond merely stating that "stress is the default." Start with a vivid, relatable sensory hook or rhetorical tension.
*   **Example:** Instead of: "In our hyper-connected, always-on world, stress has become the default setting." Try: *"Do you ever feel your mind is running ten thousand tabs at once? In our hyper-connected, always-on world, the default isn't calm—it's constant, low-grade stress."* (This makes the problem feel more immediate and personal.)

### 2. Consistency & Definition (Clarity)
*   **Objective:** Ensure that technical jargon serves the reader, rather than overwhelming them.
*   **Tweak:** When using advanced terms like "cognitive defusion," always follow the term with a parenthetical, layman's definition. This maintains the academic rigor while ensuring accessibility for the general reader.

### 3. Visual Scannability (Design)
*   **Objective:** Optimize for the modern "skim-reader."
*   **Tweak:** While the use of bolding is excellent, ensure that the *most* profound takeaway in every section is highlighted with a unique formatting element (e.g., a box quote, a different color, or a specific icon) to guide the reader's eye to the core benefit.
